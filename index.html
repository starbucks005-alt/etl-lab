Meet the ETL
Director

Dr. Terry L. Oroszi is the founder and director of the Emerging Technologies Laboratory at Wright State University's Boonshoft School of Medicine — where human judgment meets emerging technology.

A Forbes Technology Council member whose work spans AI critical thinking, CBRN biodefense, and graduate health sciences education, Dr. Oroszi brings a rare combination of scientific depth and strategic foresight to the question every institution is now facing: how do you keep humans in control when AI is in the room?

ETL Teaching Framework

Systematic Literature Review Workshop

A structured, data-driven SLR methodology taught to graduate students and clinicians — designed to compress months of work into a publishable manuscript

THE METHOD
Gold-Standard SLR
Accelerated

A strategic workflow integrating the scientific rigor of the Systematic Literature Review (SLR) with data-driven tools to accelerate the process from keyword search to publishable manuscript — without sacrificing rigor or reproducibility.

Taught as a Grand Rounds workshop to graduate students, PharmD candidates, and physician researchers at BSOM. Uses live data from PubMed, pivot tables, and mind maps to teach argument structure, research gap identification, and journal targeting in a single session.

THREE WORKSHOP TRACKS
TRACK 1
Human-Led, Minimal AI

The original classroom method. Excel and PubMed, human judgment at every step. No AI assistance. Full scientific rigor. This is where researchers learn what the process actually is before they accelerate it.

TRACK 2
AI-Assisted

Human-led with AI tools augmenting the process. AI assists with keyword generation, gap identification, and literature mapping. The researcher retains full control of every interpretive decision.

TRACK 3
AI-Accelerated + Guided Draft

Full SLR Studio pipeline. Keyword architecture, live database search, foundation paper identification, gap analysis, and AI-generated manuscript draft. All three tracks also include the Mine the Gaps mode. The zeros are not failures. They are the point.
