For Faculty
SLR Studio Is AI‑Assisted.
Not AI‑Authored.

Students still think, decide, interpret, and write. SLR Studio provides structure, evidence, and learning scaffolds. It is a teaching tool, not a writing shortcut.

1. Students Do the Intellectual Work

SLR Studio requires students to build a defensible keyword architecture, conduct a systematic search, examine subgroup patterns, identify foundation papers, interpret gaps, make decisions about relevance and inclusion, and understand how the literature fits together. These are the core cognitive tasks faculty expect at the graduate level. The tool forces them, not bypasses them.

2. Writing Is Grounded in the Student's Actual Sources

Generic LLMs hallucinate citations. SLR Studio avoids this because students supply their own PDFs, the system extracts real metadata, all tables are built from actual retrieved papers, and any generated text must cite from the student's evidence set. This ensures traceability and academic integrity.

3. Section-Based Scaffolding, Not "Write My Paper"

SLR Studio never produces a full paper from a single prompt. It guides students through individual components one at a time. Students still read, interpret, select, synthesize, revise, and defend. The AI helps them express what they have already learned.

4. The Output Is a Draft, Not a Finished Product

Everything generated is editable, revisable, transparent, traceable, and based on the student's own sources. The student must refine, integrate, and defend the work. This aligns with the expectations of graduate committees and accreditation bodies.

5. Faculty Retain Full Oversight

Because the system is structured and evidence-based, faculty can see what papers were used, how the student organized the literature, where the gaps came from, how the argument was constructed, and what the AI generated versus what the student wrote. This transparency is impossible with generic LLMs.

SLR Studio is an AI-assisted learning environment, not an AI writing replacement. It strengthens research skills, reduces hallucinations, and keeps students grounded in real evidence. The student remains the thinker, the analyst, and the author.
